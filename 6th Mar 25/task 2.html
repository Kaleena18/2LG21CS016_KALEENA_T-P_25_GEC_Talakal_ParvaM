MECCA -KAABA

Mecca, officially known as Makkah al-Mukarramah, is the holiest city in Islam and is located in western Saudi Arabia. It is the birthplace of the Prophet Muhammad and the site of the Kaaba, the most sacred spot on Earth for Muslims. The city is home to the largest mosque in the world, the Great Mosque of Mecca, and is the destination for the annual Hajj pilgrimage, which all able-bodied and financially capable Muslims are required to undertake at least once in their lifetime.

MADINAH - MUNAWWARAH

(Madinah Munawwarah) is one of Islam's holiest cities, located in Saudi Arabia. It is home to Al-Masjid an-Nabawi, the Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) mosque, where he is also buried. Medina was the destination of the Hijrah (migration) and became the first Islamic state. The city holds historical significance with sites like Quba Mosque (the first mosque in Islam), Mount Uhud (site of the Battle of Uhud), and Al-Baqi Cemetery, where many companions of the Prophet are buried. Today, Medina is a modern religious and cultural center, attracting millions of pilgrims, with a peaceful and welcoming atmosphere.
